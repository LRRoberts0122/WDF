h1
The h1 tag is the primary header tag. It is followed by secondary headers and subheaders, such as h2, h3, h4, h5 and h6.
a
The anchor tag allows you to link to other pages and even email sites. It's also useful for linking people and buttons for downloads.
form
The form element encapsulates inputs for a form. Its attributes include post and get, depending on the function of the form.
p
The paragraph element identifies body copy within the HTML document.
meta
The meta element is one of my favorites. It includes meta-data of your document, such as the character set, keywords and descriptions, which are critical for Search Engine Optimization.
head
The head element includes things such as the meta element as well as linked documents (Cascading Style Sheets and JavaScript) and the title of the page.
div
The div tag "divides" the html page into separate sections. This especially helps with CSS if you need to style sections differently.
